## Purpose

This document prevents Huddle from mixing four different money stories:

1. investor fundraising ask;
2. grant-specific budget;
3. Oslo city-cell pilot budget;
4. actual company expenditure.

These must remain separate until a finance/controller review confirms the model.

## Current finance boundary

The current planning model includes a staged NOK 20 million foundation/pre-seed round. `[C-004]`

Huddle has not raised NOK 20 million. `[C-005]`

This means all documents must use careful wording:

- “current planning assumption”;
- “intended use of funds”;
- “subject to finance reconciliation”;
- “not yet committed capital”;
- “not yet awarded grant funding.”

Do not use:

- “Huddle has raised NOK 20 million”;
- “secured funding” unless there is a signed investment or award letter;
- “approved budget” unless approved by a finance owner;
- grant-specific budget figures copied into investor materials without reconciliation.

## Budget separation rules

### Investor budget

Investor budget should explain how capital helps Huddle reach the next evidence and product milestones.

It should not imply that grant money is secured unless it is documented.

Required labels:

- stage;
- runway assumption;
- hiring/contractor assumptions;
- product milestones;
- validation and pilot milestones;
- risk reserve;
- dependency on grants or second close, if any.

### Grant budget

Grant budget should be application-specific.

It must follow the funder’s live rules around:

- applicant type;
- eligible costs;
- own contribution;
- reporting;
- procurement;
- payroll;
- subcontractors;
- VAT/accounting treatment;
- project start and end dates.

Direct grant eligibility depends on whether the route supports an ordinary Norwegian AS. `[C-024]`

Some routes may require NGO or municipality partner participation. `[C-025]`

No grant-specific budget should be submitted before live eligibility verification.

### Oslo pilot budget

Pilot budget should answer what it costs to produce decision-quality evidence in one city-cell.

It should include:

- research and consent setup;
- city-cell selection;
- organiser onboarding;
- parent recruitment;
- MVP/prototype operations;
- support and moderation;
- privacy/legal review;
- measurement and reporting;
- local content/community activation;
- contingency.

The pilot budget should not be treated as the full company operating plan.

### Actual expenditure

Actual expenditure should be recorded separately from all forecasts and asks.

Actual expenditure requires:

- date;
- vendor/person;
- category;
- amount;
- currency;
- VAT/tax treatment if relevant;
- approving owner;
- document/receipt link;
- whether the cost is grant-eligible, investor-funded or founder-funded.

## Revenue assumption control

Planned revenue layers include marketplace revenue, activity fees, expert commission and B2B revenue. These are planned revenue layers, not proven revenue. `[C-018]`

Each revenue line should be labelled as one of:

- hypothesis;
- planned for MVP;
- planned after pilot;
- requires partner contract;
- requires technical integration;
- validated by pilot;
- actual revenue.

Do not include revenue as “proven” until transaction or contract evidence exists.

## Finance QA checklist

Before any external finance wording is used:

- Has the document labelled the number as actual, forecast, budget, ask or assumption?
- Is NOK 20m described only as a planning assumption unless committed capital exists?
- Are grants described only as target routes unless awarded?
- Are investor, grant and pilot budgets separated?
- Are revenue streams clearly labelled as planned, not proven?
- Does the document avoid implying national scale has been validated?
- Has privacy/legal cost been included?
- Has Trust & Safety cost been included?
- Has design/brand/product cost been included separately?
- Has a finance/controller owner approved the wording?
