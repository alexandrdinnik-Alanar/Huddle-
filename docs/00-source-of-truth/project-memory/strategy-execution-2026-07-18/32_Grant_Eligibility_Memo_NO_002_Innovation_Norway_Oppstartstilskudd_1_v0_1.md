Recommendation: prepare, but do not submit yet.

## Huddle fit

Strong fit if the application focuses on:

- Oslo-first validation;
- testing paying or committed demand from organisers/partners;
- family problem interviews;
- organiser willingness to maintain accurate supply data;
- early market validation for a family participation platform;
- evidence-before-scale. `[C-010]`

Weak fit if positioned as:

- a social-impact grant without commercial market logic;
- finished MVP development;
- broad marketing;
- already validated demand;
- already secured partner/municipality traction without evidence.

## Suggested project framing

Working project title:

Huddle Oslo Market Validation — Testing demand for trusted family participation infrastructure

Core question:

Is there sufficient family and organiser willingness in one Oslo city-cell to justify MVP build and a controlled pilot?

Possible work packages:

1. Parent/caregiver market interviews.
2. Organiser and supply-side interviews.
3. Prototype-based willingness-to-use testing.
4. Oslo city-cell selection.
5. Market and business-model validation memo.

## Budget fit

This is a small validation grant route. The budget should be narrow.

Potential eligible-style cost categories to verify before application:

- customer/market validation work;
- prototype or concept testing;
- business-model clarification;
- market interviews;
- external support for validation.

Costs to treat carefully:

- broad marketing;
- full platform build;
- ordinary operations;
- national expansion;
- unapproved founder time assumptions;
- costs already covered by other public support.

Innovation Norway requires disclosure of whether other public support has been or will be sought for the project. Its official page also states that support can be reduced or fall away if other public support, including SkatteFUNN, later covers the same project costs.
