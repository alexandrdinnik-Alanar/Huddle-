## 2. Project title

Working title:

Huddle Oslo Market Validation — Testing demand for trusted family participation infrastructure

Alternative shorter title:

Huddle Oslo Family Participation Validation

## 3. Short project summary

Huddle is building trusted family participation infrastructure, starting with one controlled Oslo city-cell before broader build-out.

The project will test whether families and organisers show enough willingness, trust and practical demand to justify MVP build and a controlled pilot.

The work is focused on market validation, not national launch.

## 4. Problem

Families often live near activities, services, public programmes, community groups and useful family resources, but still struggle to participate.

Information is fragmented across municipal pages, club websites, private chats, social groups, registration systems, marketplaces and word-of-mouth. Parents must compare age fit, schedule, price, location, trust, equipment needs and accessibility across many disconnected sources.

The burden is highest for families with limited local networks, lower budgets, language barriers, accessibility needs, recent relocation or complex schedules.

The missing layer is not more information. The missing layer is trusted fit:

- what is relevant for this family;
- why it fits;
- who is behind it;
- what it costs;
- what data must be shared;
- what happens next;
- whether the family has a reason to return.

## 5. Innovation

Huddle is not positioned as another event directory, marketplace or parenting app.

The innovation hypothesis is a trusted family participation layer that connects:

Family context → discover → understand → trust → act → attend → return

The model is built around:

- adult-controlled family participation; `[C-015]`
- no public child profiles by default as a planned principle; `[C-017]`
- structured family-context discovery;
- organiser and programme trust signals;
- local activities, groups, services and family goods;
- privacy-safe aggregated public-value insight as a future hypothesis. `[C-019]`

For Oppstartstilskudd 1, the innovation should be framed as a market-validation hypothesis, not a completed product claim.

## 6. Market potential

Huddle’s first market test is Oslo. `[C-006]`

The first proof point is not broad national scale. It is whether one dense local city-cell can produce enough:

- family demand;
- organiser willingness;
- trust;
- repeat intent;
- practical participation signal;
- potential commercial willingness.

If validated, the model could later be repeated across other Norwegian cities and adapted to Nordic/European family participation markets.

This is a future scale hypothesis and must not be presented as proven. `[C-010]` `[C-020]`

## 7. Validation objective

The purpose of the project is to clarify whether there is a paying or committed market for Huddle’s first Oslo city-cell.

The validation should answer:

1. Do families experience the problem frequently enough?
2. Which family segments have the strongest need?
3. Will families share limited family context if the value and safety model are clear?
4. Will organisers maintain accurate activity/supply data?
5. Which organiser categories show willingness to pay, partner or continue?
6. Which Oslo neighbourhoods can support the first city-cell?
7. What is the smallest credible MVP scope?
8. Which claims must remain hypotheses until pilot evidence exists?

## 8. Work packages

### WP1 — Parent/caregiver market interviews

Planned work:

- 40–60 structured interviews; `[C-007]`
- segment families by stage, context and friction;
- test current workarounds;
- test willingness to use Huddle;
- identify trust/privacy objections;
- rank problem severity.

Output:

- parent problem ranking;
- segment priority;
- trust and privacy requirements;
- MVP must-have list.

### WP2 — Organiser and supply-side conversations

Planned work:

- 20–30 organiser/supply conversations; `[C-008]`
- test willingness to maintain listings;
- test data-quality requirements;
- understand acquisition pain;
- test willingness to pay or partner;
- identify first supply categories.

Output:

- organiser readiness ranking;
- first supply shortlist;
- commercial objections;
- data model requirements.

### WP3 — Public-value and partner signal

Planned work:

- 10–20 municipality, NGO or public-value conversations; `[C-009]`
- test public-value framing;
- assess potential partner route;
- define privacy-safe reporting boundaries;
- identify city-cell selection factors.

Output:

- public-value fit memo;
- partner-route notes;
- city-cell candidate criteria.

### WP4 — Prototype and concept testing

Planned work:

- test simple prototype or clickable concept;
- compare Huddle flow against existing discovery methods;
- test clarity of TrustMatch-style explanation;
- test what information families need before action;
- test friction points before MVP build.

Output:

- prototype feedback;
- conversion-friction map;
- MVP scope recommendation.

### WP5 — Validation decision memo

Planned work:

- summarise evidence;
- update assumptions;
- define GO / ITERATE / STOP-PAUSE recommendation;
- prepare next-step budget and pilot plan.

Output:

- day-90 decision memo;
- MVP/pilot recommendation;
- updated finance assumptions.

## 9. Expected result

The expected result is not a fully launched product.

The expected result is decision-quality evidence showing whether Huddle should:

- proceed into MVP build and Oslo city-cell pilot preparation;
- iterate segment/geography/scope/trust model;
- stop or pause because assumptions are too weak.
