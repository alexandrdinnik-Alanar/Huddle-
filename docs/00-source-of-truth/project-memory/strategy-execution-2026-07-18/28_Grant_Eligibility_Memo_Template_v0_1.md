QA rule: every completed memo requires live official-source verification before outreach or submission

## Purpose

This template standardises how Huddle evaluates grants, public funding routes, foundations and partner-route opportunities.

The goal is to avoid wasting time on applications where Huddle has the wrong applicant type, wrong project scope, wrong timing, wrong budget logic or unsupported claims.

## Non-negotiable rule

Grant eligibility must be checked route by route against live official sources.

Direct grant eligibility depends on whether the route supports an ordinary Norwegian AS. `[C-024]`

Some routes may require NGO, municipality or other partner participation. `[C-025]`

Huddle’s planned operating/applicant company is ALANAR AS, not yet incorporated. `[C-002]` `[C-003]`

## 1. Opportunity summary

Write a short factual summary:

- what the funder supports;
- what stage of project it is meant for;
- who can apply;
- why it may or may not fit Huddle;
- whether it funds validation, MVP, pilot, research, inclusion, digital innovation, sustainability or scaling.

Do not add sales language here. This section is for decision clarity.

## 2. Applicant eligibility

Answer:

1. Can an ordinary Norwegian AS apply directly?
2. If yes, are there company age, revenue, size, industry or ownership limits?
3. If no, can Huddle participate through:
   - municipality;
   - NGO/nonprofit;
   - research partner;
   - consortium;
   - subcontractor role;
   - supplier role?
4. Does the applicant need to be incorporated before application?
5. Does the applicant need Altinn, bank account, audited accounts, tax certificate or other documentation?
6. Are foreign ownership, founder status or company formation details relevant?

## 4. Budget fit

Check:

- minimum and maximum grant amount;
- percentage of eligible cost covered;
- match funding requirement;
- payroll eligibility;
- contractor eligibility;
- software development eligibility;
- marketing/community activation eligibility;
- legal/privacy/security eligibility;
- research/evaluation eligibility;
- equipment/event cost eligibility;
- VAT/accounting rules;
- payment schedule;
- reporting requirements.

Do not copy investor use-of-funds into a grant budget without adapting it to the funder’s rules.

## Completion checklist

A grant eligibility memo is complete only when:

- official URL is saved;
- access date is recorded;
- applicant type is verified;
- deadline is verified;
- eligible/ineligible costs are verified;
- required attachments are listed;
- budget logic is reviewed;
- claims are checked;
- privacy/legal risks are flagged;
- founder/funding owner has approved the recommendation.
