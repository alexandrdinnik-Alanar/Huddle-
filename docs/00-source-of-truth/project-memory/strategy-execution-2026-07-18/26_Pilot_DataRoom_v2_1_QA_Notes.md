## Evidence boundary

The documents deliberately use controlled language:

- “designed to”
- “may”
- “pilot should measure”
- “planned”
- “hypothesis”
- “requires review”
- “not approved for external use”

This is correct for the current project stage because Huddle does not yet have measured pilot evidence, completed legal/applicant setup, final finance reconciliation or legal/privacy approval.

## Strong points

- The pilot proposal now has a clear decision question and GO / ITERATE / STOP-PAUSE logic.
- The city-cell concept is framed as an operating unit, not a marketing region.
- Measurement focuses on decision-quality signals, not vanity metrics.
- The public-sector data boundary is explicit: aggregated reporting only, no child/family dossiers.
- The data-room index separates audience, status, owner, access level and release requirements.
- Superseded materials are no longer treated as active source of truth.
- Final brand package is protected as the current brand source.

## Remaining blockers

These items block external release:

1. Founder approval of wording, positioning and partner asks.
2. ALANAR AS incorporation/applicant status evidence.
3. Legal/applicant route for grants and partner agreements.
4. Finance model reconciliation and separate investor/grant/pilot budgets.
5. Privacy/legal review, especially around child data, consent, DPIA and reporting.
6. Named owner for research, operations, Trust & Safety, finance and funding.
7. Live verification of grant eligibility and deadlines from official funder sources.
8. Research protocol and consent templates before parent/organiser interviews.
9. Technical/security baseline before real family data collection.
10. Designed PDF exports using final brand guidelines.
