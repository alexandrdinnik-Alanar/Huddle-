# Huddle Short Public Overview — Outline v0.1

Date: 2026-07-18  
Status: structure draft  
Source: Huddle Master Project Bible v0.2  
Target length: 10–15 slides/pages  
Audience: parents, public readers, early partners, community members, press contacts  
External status: not ready until claims, legal/privacy and founder review

## Purpose

This is the simple version of Huddle for people who should understand the project quickly without reading the full Master Bible or a long deck.

It should answer:

- What is Huddle?
- Why is it needed?
- Who is it for?
- How does it help families?
- What are we building first?
- How can someone join, support or follow the project?

## Tone

Clear, warm, human and non-technical.

Avoid:

- investor jargon;
- technical architecture;
- too much funding language;
- excessive statistics;
- overclaiming impact.

## Proposed structure

### Slide/Page 1 — Cover

Title:

Huddle Family

Subtitle:

A trusted local place for family life.

### Slide/Page 2 — The problem

Families are surrounded by information, but finding the right local activity, support, event or children’s item is still too fragmented.

### Slide/Page 3 — What families need

Families need one trusted place to discover what fits their child, schedule, budget, location and stage of life.

### Slide/Page 4 — What Huddle is

Huddle helps families discover activities, events, trusted organisers, family services, community groups and useful children’s goods.

### Slide/Page 5 — How it works

Family context → discover → understand → trust → act → attend → return.

### Slide/Page 6 — Built around trust

Huddle is adult-controlled and designed to avoid public child profiles by default.

Claims:

C-015, C-017.

### Slide/Page 7 — Local participation

The first goal is to help families move from searching to actually joining suitable local activities.

### Slide/Page 8 — Marketplace and sustainable choices

Families can eventually find new, pre-owned and rental options that support family life and reduce cost/waste.

### Slide/Page 9 — For organisers and partners

Huddle helps local organisers, brands and family services reach the families they are meant to serve.

### Slide/Page 10 — For municipalities and communities

Huddle can help local partners understand participation barriers through privacy-safe, aggregated insight.

Claims:

C-019, C-020.

### Slide/Page 11 — Oslo first

Huddle starts with one controlled Oslo city-cell before broader growth.

Claims:

C-001.

### Slide/Page 12 — What happens next

Validation, waitlist, family interviews, organiser conversations, MVP scope and pilot preparation.

### Slide/Page 13 — Join the waitlist

Call to action:

Join as a parent, organiser, partner, advisor or supporter.

### Slide/Page 14 — Closing

Families should not have to navigate local life alone.
