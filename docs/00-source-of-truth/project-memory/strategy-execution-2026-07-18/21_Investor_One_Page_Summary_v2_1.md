## Investment thesis

Huddle is building trusted family participation infrastructure, starting in Oslo.

The company is not trying to launch another generic parenting app, marketplace, event directory or social feed. Huddle is designed as a family-first infrastructure layer that helps parents move from fragmented digital search to trusted local participation: finding what fits, understanding why it fits, connecting safely through adults, attending and returning.

The first proof is deliberately narrow: one dense Oslo city-cell that can demonstrate repeat family participation, organiser value and manageable trust-and-safety operations. `[C-001]` `[C-006]` `[C-010]`

## Problem

Family life is operationally fragmented.

A parent trying to find a suitable activity, local group, family service, expert, public programme or piece of children’s equipment must search across municipal pages, club websites, private chats, social groups, registration systems, marketplaces and recommendations.

The missing layer is not more information. The missing layer is trusted fit:

- what is relevant for this child and family;
- who is behind the activity, service or listing;
- what it costs;
- whether it is accessible;
- what needs to be shared;
- what happens next;
- whether the family has a reason to return.

Families with limited local networks, lower budgets, language barriers, accessibility needs, recent relocation, shared-care schedules or complex family realities carry the highest burden.

## Market and timing context

The external context is strong, but it does not by itself prove demand for Huddle.

- In Norway, 94% of children aged 9–11 had their own mobile phone in 2024. `[C-011]`
- Medietilsynet reported that 72% of Norwegian 9–12-year-olds use social media. `[C-027]`
- WHO Europe reported that problematic social-media use among adolescents in the WHO European region increased from 7% in 2018 to 11% in 2022. `[C-012]`
- SSB reporting indicates that organised sports participation in Norway is higher among children aged 6–11 than 12–15: 78% vs 64%. `[C-026]`
- SSB also reports income-linked participation barriers and cost/equipment/travel barriers for families. `[C-013]` `[C-014]`

These signals point to a broader family-life gap: limiting harmful digital experiences does not automatically create attractive, trusted and affordable real-world alternatives.

## Product

Huddle connects the family participation chain:

Family context → discover → understand → trust → act → attend → return

The platform is designed around:

- adult-managed family context;
- activity and event discovery;
- clear fit explanations;
- trusted organiser profiles;
- adult-controlled contact and action paths;
- reminders and return loops;
- marketplace/reuse support for family goods;
- local experts and family services;
- privacy-first public-value insight for municipalities;
- trust, safety, moderation and reporting.

Children should not become public searchable profiles by default, and unknown adults should not contact children directly. `[C-015]` `[C-016]` `[C-017]`

## First market test

Huddle will validate one Oslo city-cell before broader build-out. `[C-001]`

The first 90 days are designed to produce decision-quality evidence:

- 40–60 parent/caregiver interviews; `[C-007]`
- 20–30 organiser and supply conversations; `[C-008]`
- 10–20 municipality, NGO or public-value partner conversations; `[C-009]`
- trust, privacy and safety model testing;
- selection of one to three candidate Oslo neighbourhoods;
- frozen MVP and pilot scope;
- preliminary Trust & Safety and privacy baseline.

The day-90 decision should be GO / ITERATE / STOP-PAUSE, not automatic continuation.

## Business model

Revenue is staged behind demonstrated family and organiser value.

Planned revenue layers include:

- organiser tools and subscriptions;
- activity/event commissions or booking-related fees;
- marketplace and rental fees;
- affiliate and partner commerce;
- clearly labelled brand campaigns;
- expert/service commissions;
- municipality/public-value pilots and contracts;
- later B2B partner tools and integrations.

These are planned revenue layers, not proven revenue. `[C-018]`

## Funding plan

Current planning assumption: a staged NOK 20 million foundation round combining investor capital, grants, strategic partners and a possible second close. `[C-004]`

Huddle has not raised NOK 20 million. `[C-005]`

Capital is intended to support:

- validation;
- MVP and mobile delivery;
- privacy, Trust & Safety and legal review;
- product design and engineering;
- organiser onboarding;
- Oslo pilot operations;
- evidence production;
- core team and operations.

Use-of-funds requires finance model reconciliation before investor circulation.

## Why this can become large

If Huddle proves repeat participation and trusted local density in Oslo, the model can be repeated across Norwegian cities and later adapted to the Nordics and Europe.

The long-term category is broader than activities. Huddle can become the trusted family context layer across participation, community, services, equipment, public programmes and local commerce.

## Current investor question

Does the Oslo city-cell validation programme provide a credible path to prove the first repeatable unit of family participation infrastructure?
