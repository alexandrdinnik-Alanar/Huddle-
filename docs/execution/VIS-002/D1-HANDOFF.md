# VIS-002 — Design Lab Sprint D1 Handoff

## Scope

Created an isolated browser-based design lab at `/design-lab`.

No production route, product feature, database schema, authentication, authorization, or external dependency was changed.

## Concepts

### A — Nordic Editorial

- calm editorial composition;
- expressive serif-led hierarchy;
- warm paper, copper and forest palette;
- asymmetrical poster-like hero;
- restrained tactile geometry;
- premium, quiet and family-oriented.

### B — Local Discovery Atlas

- map-led local discovery;
- dense but structured information;
- strong utility typography;
- location markers, districts and route graphics;
- urban Oslo character;
- activity discovery is the primary visual language.

### C — Human Community Journal

- community storytelling;
- warmer organic composition;
- handwritten annotation details;
- family quote and human narrative;
- soft imperfection and tactile surfaces;
- emotional trust and belonging.

## Shared comparison surface

Each direction includes:

- navigation;
- homepage hero;
- search composition;
- activity cards;
- desktop and mobile responsive behavior;
- at least one trust/community signal;
- reduced-motion support.

## Evaluation criteria

Score each direction from 1–5 on:

1. distinctive Huddle identity;
2. trust for families;
3. Oslo/local relevance;
4. visual sophistication;
5. clarity and usability;
6. mobile integrity;
7. accessibility potential;
8. extensibility into app and admin surfaces;
9. resistance to generic AI/SaaS aesthetics;
10. feasibility without performance-heavy effects.

## Explicit non-decisions

- no concept is approved for production;
- no Figma production node exists yet;
- placeholder abstract visuals are art-direction devices, not final photography or illustration;
- final fonts, photography direction, iconography and motion system remain open;
- Claude prototype files remain UX references only.

## Next review

The product owner should compare A, B and C in the browser. After review, create a D2 direction that may select one concept or intentionally combine specific approved traits. Do not merge the selected direction into production routes before the Figma governance gate is complete.
